Connecting to Country

Cultural heritage, language, and ecological notes from the Tidbinbilla area.

Acknowledgement of Country

We acknowledge the Ngunnawal People as the traditional custodians of this land, the Country known today as Tidbinbilla and the ACT region. We recognise their continuing culture and the deep spiritual connection that the Ngunnawal People have maintained with this Country over more than 25,000 years. We commit to respectful engagement with Ngunnawal cultural knowledge and practices.

This website respects traditional indigenous culture and shares information in a respectful way.

Language & Connection

The Ngunnawal language carries asosciations to the land and its ecosystems. Click on any word below to learn about essential vocabulary that reflects the relationship between the Ngunnawal People and Tidbinbilla Country.

Yuma

Hello / Greetings

General welcoming statement used when entering Ngunnawal Country.

Birrigai

Place Name

The name of the Birrigai Rock Shelter, one of the oldest known Aboriginal occupation sites in the region.

Maliyan

Wedge tail eagle

A symbolic guardian spirit, creator figure

Ngunnawal

The People / The Country

term for the traditional Indigenous nations of the Canberra and ACT region.

Tidbinbilla

Tidbinbilla / "Where boys become men"

The local name for this area known for its natural landscape and ngunnawal heritage.

About This Website

This educational portal provides a simple and respectful introduction to Ngunnawal practices and knowledge systems centred on Tidbinbilla Country. The website is organised around three main areas of cultural knowledge:

Significant Cultural Sites

A map of marked sites in the Tidbinbilla area with cultural and ecological information.

Seasonal Ecological Knowledge

Information on the four seasons around Tidbinbilla, with details about weather, plants, animals, and seasonal activities.

Language & Connection

Engage with essential Ngunnawal vocabulary that demonstrates the integrated relationship between language, land, and living systems.
